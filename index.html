Services We offer

Medical Coding

Medical coding involves assigning standardized codes to the diagnoses, procedures, and services provided by healthcare providers. This ensures accurate and consistent documentation of medical services, which is essential for billing and reimbursement purposes

Claims Submission

After medical coding, we submit claims electronically or through paper-based processes to insurance companies or other third-party payers on behalf of healthcare providers. This includes verifying claim information, checking for errors, and following up on any rejected or denied claims.

Insurance Verification

Insurance verification involves verifying the eligibility and coverage details of patients' insurance plans to ensure that the services provided by healthcare providers are covered and reimbursable. This helps prevent claim denials and delays in reimbursement.

Payment Posting

Payment posting involves recording and reconciling payments received from insurance companies, patients, and other payers. This includes posting payments to the appropriate patient accounts, identifying and resolving discrepancies, and managing adjustments and write-offs.

Denial Management

Denial management involves identifying and resolving claim denials to ensure maximum reimbursement. This includes investigating the reasons for denials, appealing denied claims, and resolving any coding or documentation issues to prevent future denials.

Accounts Receivable (AR) Follow-up

AR follow-up involves monitoring and managing outstanding claims and unpaid invoices, following up with insurance companies and patients to ensure timely payment, and resolving any billing or coding issues that may be delaying reimbursement.

Patient Billing and Collections

Patient billing and collections involve generating patient statements, managing patient billing inquiries, setting up payment plans, and collecting payments from patients. This includes providing clear and comprehensive explanations of charges, insurance coverage, and payment options to patients.

Financial Reporting

Financial reporting involves generating regular reports that provide healthcare providers with insights into their revenue cycle performance, including key metrics such as collections, aging of accounts receivable, and claim submission and payment trends. This helps providers monitor their financial health and make informed decisions.

Compliance and Regulatory Support

Compliance and regulatory support involve ensuring that the medical billing practices and processes comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and guidelines, such as HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and Medicare regulations.
